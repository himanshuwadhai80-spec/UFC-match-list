The UFC is responsible for professionalizing MMA, turning it from a spectacle of "style vs. style" into a globally recognized sport with strict weight classes, medical testing, and unified rules.

History & Evolution

The UFC was founded by Art Davie and Rorion Gracie and held its first event, UFC 1, in Denver, Colorado, on November 12, 1993. The original goal was to determine which martial art was superior in a situation with minimal rules. It was marketed as a "no-holds-barred" tournament.

In 2001, Zuffa, LLC (led by Dana White and the Fertitta brothers) purchased the struggling promotion. They transformed it from a controversial spectacle into a sanctioned, regulated sport by working with athletic commissions to implement the Unified Rules of Mixed Martial Arts. Today, it is the premier MMA organization in the world.
